The author of this page is a former teacher. Safe-guarding advice for UK teacher-training is clear: Do not promise to keep a confidence that you might not be able to, but stress that you will not automatically inform a child's parent or guardian of anything, if it's not in the best interests of the child. The author considers it fortunate that they started teaching after the repeal of Section 28, which would have made it very difficult to intervene in homophobic bullying. Any requirement to notify parents of gender non-conforming behaviour is against best-practice, and a return to those times. Following a leak that the UK Government does indeed intend to do this, there is a petition to request that it doesn't. As ever, the UK media is not helping, with easily de-bunked stories about pupils "Identifing as cats, horses and dinosaurs", rather like the US litter boxes hoax.
